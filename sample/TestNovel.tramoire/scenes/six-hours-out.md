She had been driving for six hours and the radio had given up somewhere past the county line. The road did what roads do out here — it went straight, and then it went straight again. Ines Adair had stopped expecting it to do anything else.

Marcus Vale slept against the window with his jacket bunched under his cheek, which meant he would wake up *angry about his neck* and pretend it was about something else.

The letter was in the glovebox, still folded the way Nadia Adair had folded it. Ines had not read past the second line. She was aware, in the way you are aware of weather, that this would have to change **before morning**.
